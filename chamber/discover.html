Discover More About Hilo Hawaii

Hilo is full of sacred areas. People came to Hilo around 1100 AD. They set up fishing spots and built up the land. Many of the fish ponds are very sacred and not for swimming. Hawaiian culture is all about showing your gratitude to the land. They fish and farm and are very grateful for the food the land has provided for them. Hilo is a very creative area full of many centers for the arts. Hula, pottery, paddling and many other fun and creative activities. Hilo is in the jungle of Hawaii. Most beaches are black sand and cold water. There are so many waterfalls that most have not even been found yet. The population of Hilo was 45,248 in 2020.

#3 - Hilo Bay took 22 years to build. It was finished in 1930 to help shield the boats in the bay from the rough ocean waves.

#4

#4 - The Lili'uokalani gardens were named after the last ruling monarch in Hawaii, Queen Lili'uokalani. The park is full of beautiful Japanese architecture.

#5

#5 - Rainbow falls is a waterfall that falls over a lava cave known in Hawaiian legends as the home of the goddess Hina. It is called rainbow falls because most mornings, the mist from the falls will create a rainbow. Rainbow falls is part of the Wailuku River.
